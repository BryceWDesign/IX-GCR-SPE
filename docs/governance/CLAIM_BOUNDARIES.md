# Claim Boundaries

IX-GCR-SPE is allowed to be ambitious. It is not allowed to blur the line
between direction, model result, and validated hardware.

## Claims the repository may make

- hydrogen-rich, low-Z materials are the baseline passive direction,
- local storm shelters are more credible than uniform whole-vehicle thickening,
- a small crew Shadow Vault can be a better mass use than evenly spreading the
  same mass across a large cabin,
- shield health monitoring materially improves trustworthiness and degraded-mode
  survivability,
- optional local active magnetic augmentation is worth bounded study,
- public tools such as OLTARIS and Geant4 are appropriate verification paths.

## Claims the repository may not make without new evidence

- 100 percent protection from GCR in free space,
- “radiation solved,”
- diamond as a proven bulk crew shield,
- active magnetic shielding as low-risk or low-mass,
- any medical risk reduction percentage not tied to a stated model and geometry,
- any flight-readiness statement.

## Language rules

Use:
- “design direction”
- “trade-study branch”
- “illustrative configuration”
- “hypothesis”
- “modeled target”
- “not yet validated”

Do not use:
- “guaranteed”
- “complete protection”
- “radiation proof”
- “solves GCR”
- “crew safe at all costs”

## Diamond-specific rule

Diamond may be discussed here as:
- detector material,
- dosimetry material,
- harsh-environment sensing material,
- specialty tribology or wear layer.

Diamond may not be described here as the main bulk crew-shield answer unless
transport evidence for the exact geometry, spectra, and areal density is added.

## Active-shielding rule

Local superconducting magnetic augmentation is retained only as an optional
branch around the Shadow Vault. Vehicle-wide active shielding remains out of
baseline scope because of mass, stored-energy, quench, structure, and recovery
burdens.

## Thick-shielding rule

Increasing thickness is not treated as automatically beneficial beyond moderate
areal densities because the mixed field behind thick shielding can become
dominated by secondary particles. Any thick-shield branch beyond 100 g/cm²
requires explicit transport justification.

## Repository honesty rule

If the geometry, materials, fill state, or event environment are unknown,
the repository must report uncertainty rather than nominal performance.
