# Shadow Vault Concept

## Core idea

The Shadow Vault is a **small, intensely protected internal crew refuge**.
It is the place where the best shielding, best monitoring, and optional local
active augmentation all converge.

## Why a vault instead of a uniformly shielded cabin

Because the same mass used uniformly often protects empty air.
The vault approach protects:
- the crew,
- the most occupied zone,
- the place they can rapidly retreat to during SPE,
- the sleeping and command positions most tied to survival.

## Vault functions

- storm shelter for acute SPE events
- high-occupancy sleep and rest zone
- command refuge during high radiation alerts
- anchor point for local dosimetry and optional magnetic augmentation

## Vault geometry principles

1. Keep the protected volume small.
2. Surround it as continuously as practical.
3. Treat hatch-path leakage as a first-class problem.
4. Integrate useful mass around it.
5. Avoid overpromising vehicle-wide equivalence.

## Candidate vault branches

### Baseline passive vault
- water-equivalent chambers around the occupied zone
- PE/UHMWPE inner boundary
- boron/nitrogen-aware graded transition

### Aggressive cryogenic vault
- annular and end-cap cryogenic hydrogen shielding
- thermal isolation layer
- PE hatch-path cover and bypass treatment

### Aggressive hydride-enhanced vault
- modular encapsulated hydride cartridges
- local enhancement where crew orientation and time-in-zone justify it

### Optional active vault
- local toroidal or open-geometry superconducting magnetic augmentation
- passive survivability retained if the field is lost

## AHIS-derived monitoring applied to the vault

The vault is monitored for:
- fill-state drift,
- leak onset,
- hatch closure and seal status,
- abnormal thermal trends,
- dosimetry anomalies,
- active-field availability in augmented branch.

## Performance posture

The Shadow Vault is expected to:
- attack SPE aggressively,
- reduce GCR more honestly than a uniform wall,
- remain truthful about degraded protection.

It is not expected to make free-space GCR disappear.
