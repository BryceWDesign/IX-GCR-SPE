# Patent Landscape

This document records public patent directions relevant to IX-GCR-SPE.
It is **not** an implementation recipe and must not be treated as a claim-copying
exercise.

## 1. Water-around-crew architecture
Public signal:
- annular or enclosing water tanks around an internal crew or structural core.

Why it matters here:
- validates water placement as a serious geometry choice,
- reinforces the idea that useful mass should surround the protected volume.

Example direction:
- US6231010B1, *Advanced structural and inflatable hybrid spacecraft module*.

## 2. Cryogenic hydrogen annulus around crew module
Public signal:
- cryogenic hydrogen in annular and toroidal vessels around a room-temperature
  crew module.

Why it matters here:
- supports the aggressive Shadow Vault branch,
- confirms the main trade is mass efficiency versus brutal thermal burden.

Example direction:
- US10144535B2, *Cryogenic hydrogen radiation shield for human spaceflight*.

## 3. Hydrogen-boron-nitrogen multifunctional shielding materials
Public signal:
- low-Z structural composites using hydrogen, boron, and nitrogen with
  attention to GCR, neutrons, and SEP.

Why it matters here:
- supports graded, multifunctional low-Z branches,
- supports BNNT-aware secondary-management layers.

Example direction:
- US10607742B2, *Radiation shielding materials containing hydrogen, boron and nitrogen*.

## 4. Polymer-bound hydrogen-rich shielding media
Public signal:
- hydrogen-rich media encapsulated, sandwiched, or bound within polymers.

Why it matters here:
- supports modular chambered panels,
- supports retained-mass and foam / binder concepts.

Example direction:
- US20140299794A1, *Spacecraft shield*.

## 5. Diamond detector and dosimeter concepts
Public signal:
- diamond used as detector material and dosimetry component.

Why it matters here:
- supports keeping diamond in the instrumentation lane,
- does not justify diamond as the primary bulk crew-shield layer.

## 6. Rules for repo use of patent material

1. Do not copy patent claims into design text.
2. Do not paraphrase patent claims as if they are original proof.
3. Use patents to identify:
   - geometry ideas,
   - failure burdens,
   - subsystem interactions,
   - terminology worth verifying in literature.
4. Keep this repo’s implementations original and explicitly high-level.
