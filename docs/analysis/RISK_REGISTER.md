# Risk Register

| ID | Risk | Why it matters | Current posture |
|---|---|---|---|
| R-01 | Overclaiming GCR protection | The field does not support literal free-space total protection. | Mitigate through claim boundaries and model discipline. |
| R-02 | Uniform-wall thinking | Wastes mass away from the most occupied zone. | Mitigate through Shadow Vault architecture. |
| R-03 | Thick-shield secondary buildup | Beyond moderate depth, the mixed field can worsen or plateau. | Require transport-model review for thick branches. |
| R-04 | Cryogenic hydrogen thermal burden | Extreme passive branch may fail on boiloff and heat leak. | Keep as aggressive branch, not baseline. |
| R-05 | Hydride handling and containment | Chemical and packaging burden may offset shielding gain. | Treat as encapsulated modular branch only. |
| R-06 | Hatch-path weakness | A strong wall can be defeated by a weak door path. | First-class hatch treatment required. |
| R-07 | Shield mass migration | Consumables or fill mass may move away from crew zone. | Add zone-level accounting and monitored fill state. |
| R-08 | Silent leak or voiding | Protection can degrade without obvious signs. | Use AHIS-derived health monitoring. |
| R-09 | Active augmentation quench | Field collapse can create abrupt loss of protection and thermal events. | Keep passive baseline survivable; define safe fallback. |
| R-10 | EMI/EMC side effects | Local fields and sensors can interfere with each other. | Active branch requires discipline and separation. |
| R-11 | Model theater | Nice figures can hide weak assumptions. | Keep assumptions and outputs explicit. |
| R-12 | Patent laundering | Public patents can tempt derivative writing. | Use patents for direction-finding only. |
| R-13 | Diamond overuse | Interesting detector material could be miscast as miracle wall. | Restrict diamond to sensing and specialty roles. |
| R-14 | Weak zone undercounting | Corners, seams, and penetrations can dominate real exposure. | Force weak-zone review in architecture docs. |
| R-15 | Orientation blindness | Radiation exposure changes with direction and vehicle attitude. | Include directional scenarios in transport plan. |
| R-16 | EVA exposure under-modeled | Crew outside the vault are much more exposed. | Include EVA alerting and abort-state logic. |
| R-17 | Source staleness | Current space-radiation design advice changes slowly but not never. | Maintain traceability map with retrieval dates. |
